Jerome's Artwork

Since I was young I've always had some creative outlet. Inspired by the rennaisance men I learned about in middle school, it's been my life goal to not only be well versed in technology but in the arts as well. This has taken many forms over the years, though woodworking will always hold a special place in my heart. I hope you enjoy looking through some of my recent work!

Yin Yang Side-table

My most recent work is this Yin Yang side table that I made from a bunch of wooden pallets my friend had laying around. I chopped up the pallets and painstakingly re-surfaced each piece of the table before glueing and cutting it to size. Over 6 hours were spent sanding it down to 1000 grit sandpaper. I bent and welded the legs as well.

Unintelligent Wooden Toilet

In my sculpture class we were tasked with creating an everyday object out of wood. After giving it some thought, I decided to do a toilet as a challenge. I created a mock up out of some scrap wood and measured each and every angle. Once that was done I fabricated each bowl-piece eight times. Then came the fun part of glueing, nailing, and screwing it all together!

Shell-chime from recycled wood

To create this piece I started with an old log. I carefully removed the bark with a chisel and then got to sanding down any marks i put in the grain to give it a drift-wood look. I then cut the log down in half and bent some aluminum wire I had into hooks. Then I drilled holes and secured the hooks with loctite. I then strung up the shells and secured the rope. It sounds great!
